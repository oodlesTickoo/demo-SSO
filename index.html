Assumptions

  •  
    The Canadian average household net worth and the average provincial net worth are taken from the Median net worth data of Statistics Canada Survey of Financial Security 2012.
  •  
    Due to data limitations we do not have net worth averages for Northwest Territories, Yukon and Nunavut.
  •  
    Your result is the value of what you own minus the value of what you owe.
  •  
    This demonstration tool is presented for information purposes only. The figures are not guaranteed, as they are based on assumptions that are certain to change.

Non-registered savings and investments

This includes other taxable non-registered savings and investment accounts such as cash, chequing and savings accounts, guaranteed investment certificates (GIC), mutual funds, annuities, stocks, bonds, etc.

Registered retirement savings

This includes all of your registered accounts such as registered retirement savings plans (RRSP), group RSPs, defined contribution pension plans (DCPP), defined benefit pension plans (DBPP), locked-in RSPs/LIRA, etc.

Close
 
 

Tax-free savings accounts (TFSA)

A TFSA lets you save for any purpose, without paying taxes on the investment growth. TFSA contribution inputs assume that you are following annual limits.

Home value

This includes a detached home, semi-detached home, townhome, apartment or condominium that you own. Enter the current value, not what you originally paid or think this asset will be worth in the future.

Other mortgages

This is the current principal balance for any other real estate mortgages you may have. This includes mortgages on cottages, vacation or rental properties and any other real estate.

Vehicle(s)

This includes the current value of vehicles such as cars, trucks, motorcycles, boats and other recreational vehicles such as snowmobiles, ATVs or RVs.

Other valuables

This includes the current value of items such as jewellery, electronics, furniture and appliances, art and other items that have a high resale value.

Home mortgage

This is the current principal balance remaining on your mortgage. It is also the amount that you would have to pay to own your home.

Other mortgages
